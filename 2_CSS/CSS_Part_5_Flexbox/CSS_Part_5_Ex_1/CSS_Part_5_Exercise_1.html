Born: January 08, 1947

David Robert Jones, better known by his stage name David Bowie, (Brixton, London, January 8, 1947 - New York, January 10, 2016) was a British singer, songwriter, music producer and actor. He is considered one of the most influential rock musicians from the sixties to the present day.

David Bowie became known through world hits such as Space Oddity, Changes, Life on Mars?, Ziggy Stardust, Let's Dance, Absolute Beginners and Heroes.

Career

Scheduled finale: January 10, 2016

Producer Tony Visconti declared after Bowie's death that the album Blackstar was intended by Bowie as a farewell gift to his fans as a carefully planned "finale". After Bowie's death Tony Visconti told in an interview with the American music magazine Rolling Stone that Bowie had told him a year ago that he was suffering from cancer, but in the summer of 2015 he heard that treatments had been well received.

However, the cancer came back later and in November 2015 Bowie knew he was incurably ill. According to Visconti, Bowie wrote five new songs in the weeks before his death and called him a week before his death to make arrangements for the recording of a next album. "Bowie must have thought he would live a few more months," says Visconti in Rolling Stone. It is still unclear whether a Bowie album will be released with unpublished new music.
